Mohammad Ali Jinnah, (Urdu: محمد علی جناح‎; Gujarati: મહંમદ અલી ઝીણા; 25 December 1876 – 11 September 1948 in Karachi) was a Pakistani politician. He is the founder of Pakistan. After the partition of India, he became the Governor-General of Pakistan. As a mark of respect, Pakistanis call him Quaid-e-Azam.
